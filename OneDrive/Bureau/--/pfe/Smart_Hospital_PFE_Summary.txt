🩺 WHAT CAN A DOCTOR CONTROL & MONITOR IN A SMART HOSPITAL SYSTEM?

1️⃣ Vital signs (real-time monitoring) Doctors can see and follow live
data: ❤️ Heart rate (HR) from ECG 🫁 SpO₂ (oxygen saturation) 🌡️ Body
temperature 📈 ECG waveform (basic or advanced) ➡️ This replaces manual
checks and saves time.

2️⃣ Automatic alerts (very important) Doctors can set thresholds for each
patient: - HR too high / too low - SpO₂ < 92% - Temperature > 38°C -
Abnormal ECG pattern

📢 Alert types: - Dashboard notification - SMS / Email / Mobile App ➡️
Doctor is alerted before the situation becomes critical.

3️⃣ Risk prediction (AI 🧠) - Predict cardiac risk (tachycardia,
bradycardia) - Predict respiratory distress - Detect early deterioration
📊 Example: “Patient has a 78% risk of hypoxia in the next 30 minutes”
➡️ Helps doctors anticipate, not just react.

4️⃣ Patient status classification 🟢 Stable 🟠 Needs attention 🔴
Critical

5️⃣ Historical data & trends - Graphs (hour/day/week) - Treatment
effectiveness analysis

6️⃣ Patient-specific control - Adjust thresholds - Medical notes -
Monitoring frequency - Priority level ➡️ Personalized medicine 💡

7️⃣ Decision support (AI-assisted) - Suggestions only (doctor decides) ⚠️
Final decision is always made by the doctor.

8️⃣ Multi-patient monitoring (ICU style) 9️⃣ Remote monitoring 🔟 Data
export & reports (PDF / CSV)

🧱 Architecture: Patient → Sensors → IoT Gateway → Backend
(Flask/FastAPI) → AI Engine → Dashboard

🔥 PFE sentence: “This system allows doctors to monitor patients in real
time, receive early alerts, and use artificial intelligence as a
decision-support tool to improve patient safety and response time.”
