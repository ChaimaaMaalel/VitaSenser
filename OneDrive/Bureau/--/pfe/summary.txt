Smart Hospital – Intelligent Patient Monitoring System

This project proposes the design and implementation of a Smart Hospital system for intelligent and continuous patient health monitoring by combining Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and software development technologies. The main objective is to assist medical staff, especially doctors, in monitoring patients in real time, detecting abnormal health conditions early, and improving decision-making while ensuring patient safety.

The system relies on IoT medical sensors installed on patients to continuously collect vital signs such as electrocardiogram (ECG), heart rate, oxygen saturation (SpO₂), and body temperature. These sensors transmit data to a central backend system through an IoT gateway. The collected data is stored, processed, and analyzed in real time, allowing continuous monitoring without the need for manual measurements.

On the medical side, the system provides doctors with a web or mobile dashboard where they can monitor multiple patients simultaneously. Doctors can visualize real-time signals, historical data, and trends through graphs and charts. The system allows doctors to define personalized thresholds for each patient, add medical notes, adjust alert parameters, and classify patient priority levels. Automatic alerts are generated when abnormal values are detected, enabling faster medical intervention and reducing the risk of critical situations.

Artificial Intelligence plays a central role in the system by enhancing traditional monitoring approaches. Instead of relying only on fixed threshold-based alerts, the proposed solution integrates machine learning models to perform anomaly detection, risk prediction, and patient status classification. These models analyze time-series medical data to detect unusual patterns, predict potential health risks such as cardiac or respiratory deterioration, and classify patients into stable, warning, or critical states. The AI models are designed as decision-support tools, meaning that the final medical decision always remains under the responsibility of the doctor.

Due to the sensitivity and limited availability of medical data, the AI models are developed using a fine-tuning approach rather than training from scratch. Publicly available and academically accepted datasets, such as those from PhysioNet and Kaggle, are used for training and validation. In addition, simulated or synthetic data may be generated to complement real datasets while respecting data privacy constraints. The models selected are lightweight and interpretable, such as Isolation Forest, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, or LSTM for time-series analysis, ensuring real-time performance and explainability.

The system is implemented using an open-source and low-cost architecture, making it suitable for academic research and adaptable to resource-limited environments. Model training can be performed on standard computers or cloud platforms such as Google Colab, without the need for high-performance hardware or GPUs. Trained models are integrated into the backend and used for real-time inference.

Although similar patient monitoring systems exist in real hospitals, the added value of this project lies in the integration of low-cost IoT devices with AI-based prediction, personalized monitoring, and explainable decision support within an academic prototype. The project focuses on innovation at the architectural and analytical levels rather than replacing certified medical equipment.

The system also acknowledges its limitations. Sensor reliability, limited datasets, potential false alerts, data privacy, cybersecurity concerns, and real-time network latency are considered as constraints. These limitations are clearly identified, and possible improvements are proposed as future work, such as advanced security mechanisms, sensor fault detection, larger datasets, and improved AI models.

In conclusion, this Smart Hospital project demonstrates how IoT, AI, and software development can be combined to create an intelligent patient monitoring system that enhances healthcare efficiency, supports medical decision-making, and improves patient safety. The proposed solution serves as a strong academic prototype that reflects real-world challenges while providing a solid foundation for future research and development in smart healthcare systems.
